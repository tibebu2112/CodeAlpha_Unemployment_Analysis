# Resilience and Crisis: A Data-Driven Analysis of India's Labor Market (2019-2020)

## 📌 Project Overview
This project was developed as part of the **CodeAlpha Data Science Internship (Task 2)**. It provides a comprehensive analytical study of the unemployment trends in India before and during the COVID-19 pandemic. By leveraging Python’s data science ecosystem, this analysis transforms raw socio-economic data into actionable insights, identifying the specific months, regions, and sectors that faced the most significant economic volatility.

## 📊 Key Objectives
* **Data Preprocessing:** Cleaning and standardizing labor market datasets.
* **COVID-19 Impact Analysis:** Quantifying the sudden spike in unemployment during the 2020 lockdown.
* **Seasonal & Sectoral Trends:** Investigating "Rural vs. Urban" disparities and monthly fluctuations.
* **Geospatial Analysis:** Identifying the most vulnerable states to inform social policy.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack
- **Language:** Python 3.x
- **Libraries:** - `Pandas` (Data Manipulation)
  - `Matplotlib` & `Seaborn` (Static Visualizations)
  - `Plotly` (Interactive Trend Charts)
  - `Datetime` (Time-series Analysis)

## 📈 Key Insights
1. **The Lockdown Shock:** National unemployment average surged from **~9.5%** to **~17.8%** within two months (March to May 2020).
2. **Peak Impact:** In April 2020, certain regions like Puducherry reached a peak unemployment rate of over **75%**.
3. **Urban vs. Rural Buffer:** Rural areas showed higher resilience due to the continued operation of agricultural activities, whereas Urban sectors faced vertical spikes due to service industry shutdowns.
4. **Seasonality:** Data indicates a recurring rise in job-seeking activity during May/June, coinciding with the academic year-end and pre-monsoon agricultural cycles.

## 💡 Policy Recommendations
Based on the data, the following social policies are suggested:
- **Urban Employment Guarantees:** Implementing schemes similar to MGNREGA in urban centers to support daily-wage workers during economic shocks.
- **Regional Support:** Prioritizing states like Haryana and Jharkhand for structural industrial reforms to address chronic high unemployment.
